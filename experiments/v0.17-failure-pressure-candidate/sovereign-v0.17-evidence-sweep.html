Interpretation — answering the disposition question K

The questions v0.17 was designed to answer:

  • Can fragile-credit escalation push through the Credit-5 floor without adding a fourth card? Yes — 2 canonical games now end below 5 (vs v0.16's 0). The floor breach is real.
  • Is the escalation timing-limited? Yes — Spec Fever -2 fires only 2 / 400 (below the 5-game timing-limit threshold). The escalation only matters when Spec Fever happens to land at credit ≤ 6, which is a small subset of when it lands at all.
  • Does Default fire? No — credit 4 is two steps from the 0-credit gate, and no recovery card pushes it further down nor lifts it back. Default's threshold is far from the live pressure band even after v0.17.

Structural finding: Below credit 5, the game state is sticky in both directions. Recovery requires a +1 card draw that doesn't push past 4→5 fast enough to escape the next pressure event, and pressure requires another -2 or compound -1 hit that doesn't happen often enough. The credit ≤ 5 band is a "frozen" floor where games park.

The conceptual question this raises (deferred): Is Default supposed to be a catastrophic near-impossible collapse at Credit 0, or should the game have a softer "credit crisis" event around Credit 4? v0.17 produces 2 games that end at credit 4, but no game-mechanical event recognizes that state. The original Default-at-0 threshold may be a left-over from a different pressure model.

Suggested v0.18 directions (if authorized — observation only)

  • v0.18 — fourth independent Credit-down source: Add a new credit-down trigger (e.g., Cotton Gin Patented could lose its Capacity boost in exchange for Credit -1, or a Whiskey Excise enforcement card could fire at lap 5+). Broadens the pressure base so more games can compound. Per the timing-limited classification rule, this is the recommended next lever.
  • v0.18 alt — soft credit-crisis at credit ≤ 4: Add an event that fires when credit is at ≤ 4 (e.g., -1 cash per turn, lockout from Republic Debate cards, or a "Credit Crisis" log row). Doesn't change Default's 0-credit threshold but makes the credit-4 state mechanically meaningful — which would make the 2 games in v0.17 actually matter for game feel.
  • v0.18 alt — lower the Default threshold: Currently Default fires at credit = 0. If lowered to credit ≤ 2, the 2 games at credit 4 still don't fire it but the threshold is closer to live pressure. Caveat: lowering thresholds is a balance lever, not a pressure lever; needs careful eval.

None of these are authorized. Each would need its own explicit kickoff.

Closeout L

v0.17 candidate is STRONGEST PRESSURE CANDIDATE — FLOOR BREACHED BUT TIMING-LIMITED. Recommended disposition: Keep v0.17 as a proof-of-concept lever; the timing-limit classification points to v0.18 = fourth independent pressure source. The recovery-gate branch (v0.14/v0.15) is retired. The pressure-side branch now has two confirmed strengths (v0.16 floor-reach via Anti-Fed Pamphlet, v0.17 floor-breach via Spec Fever escalation) and one identified weakness (timing — only 2 / 400 escalation fires in canonical).
