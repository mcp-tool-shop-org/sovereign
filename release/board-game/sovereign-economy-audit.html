Economy Math Audit · Corrected

Corrected expected-value model · replaces FC-EM-001 in full

The prior audit (FC-EM-001) contained four math errors that flipped most of its quantitative findings. This document recomputes the economy from first principles, shows every formula with numbers plugged in, and lists which prior conclusions survived and which did not.

§ 01Verdict

Tight · upgrade-gated

No system in Sovereign pays back its purchase from rent alone, even with its Act applied. The economy works only when players upgrade — and Tier I is the hardest payback gate to clear, not the safest.

The single corrected number that drives most other findings: per-property landings over the entire 7-lap game are 2.0 in 3-player and 3.0 in 4-player. Multiplied by base rents that top out at 50 TN per landing, no set-only return exceeds its purchase cost. The previous audit's table reporting ROI > 1.0 for seven of eight systems was wrong: it applied Act multipliers across all 7 laps and treated per-lap landings inconsistently with per-game landings.

Routes are the surprise. Owning all 4 routes returns ~ 1 600 TN in 3-player and ~ 2 400 TN in 4-player against 800 TN cost — the only asset class that pays back as a set without upgrades. Strategic Industry's Tier I is the fastest tier payback in the game, not a late-game trap. Treasury / Finance is not dominant in 3-player; Merchant / Infrastructure with all four routes wins.

§ 04Upgrade Payback · Incremental

Each tier's payback is computed against the incremental rent over the previous state, not against total tier rent. Increments per landing per property:

Set → Tier I : base × (5 − 2) = base × 3 Tier I → II : base × (15 − 5) = base × 10 Tier II → III : base × (30 − 15) = base × 15 Income per lap (set-wide) = increment × landings/lap × N_properties Payback laps = tier_cost / income_per_lap Where tier_cost = "Upgrade" value × N_properties (full-set upgrade).

§ 05Bank & Mint · Corrected

Bank of the United States · pre-charter (laps 1–2) and post-charter (laps 3–7)

pre = 4 × dice × landings/lap × 2 = 28 × landings/lap × 2 post = 10 × dice × landings/lap × 5 = 70 × landings/lap × 5 3p: pre = 28 × 0.286 × 2 = 16.00; post = 70 × 0.286 × 5 = 100.10; total = 116.10 TN 4p: pre = 28 × 0.429 × 2 = 24.02; post = 70 × 0.429 × 5 = 150.15; total = 174.17 TN cost = 150 TN ROI 3p = 0.774 (below break-even) · ROI 4p = 1.161 (just above)

Mint · three states

Assume player owns Bank lap 1 and Mint lap 2. State at each lap depends on Bank Charter (lap 3) and dual-Institution ownership.

State 1 (Mint alone, no Bank) : 0 TN/land · lap 1 only (Mint not yet bought) — n/a State 2 (Both, no Charter) : 10 × 7 = 70 TN/land · lap 2 only (Charter is lap 3) State 3 (Both + Charter) : 20 × 7 = 140 TN/land · laps 3–7 (5 laps) 3p: state 2 = 70 × 0.286 × 1 = 20.02; state 3 = 140 × 0.286 × 5 = 200.20; total = 220.22 TN 4p: state 2 = 70 × 0.429 × 1 = 30.03; state 3 = 140 × 0.429 × 5 = 300.30; total = 330.33 TN cost = 150 TN ROI 3p = 1.468 · ROI 4p = 2.202
Bank is borderline in 3-player (does not pay back its purchase from rent alone). Mint clears it easily — but only because the Bank is already paid for. The Bank + Mint stack as a single decision pays back fully (~ 340 TN rent vs 300 TN cost in 3p). Buying Mint without Bank is dead capital.

§ 11Recommendations

R-01 · Coinage Act buff survives

Current: Mint owner collects 25 TN per other player. Public Credit +1.

Proposed: Mint owner collects 50 TN per other player. Public Credit +1. Industrial Capacity +1.

Formula: Coinage at 25 × (N−1) yields 50 – 75 TN of one-time cash; Assumption yields ~ 100 TN of permanent rent and Bank Charter yields ~ 240 TN. Doubling Coinage brings it to 100 – 150 TN and adds Capacity momentum.

Confidence: high. Survives corrected math.

R-02 · Industrial Capacity threshold lower survives

Current: ≥ 8 = +25 % Mfg/Strategic payments. ≥ 10 = +2 Influence to those owners at game end.

Proposed: ≥ 6 = +25 %. ≥ 8 = +2 Inf bonus.

Formula: Capacity reaches 3–5 in expected play; threshold 8 reached in ~ 15–20 % of games; threshold 10 unreachable without targeted card draws. Lowering both thresholds by 2 aligns with the track's actual movement range.

Confidence: high.

R-03 · Strategic Industry pricing does NOT survive

The prior audit proposed reducing Armory 350 → 300 and Shipbuilding 400 → 350 on the theory that Strategic Industry is a late-game trap. Corrected math shows Strategic Industry has the best Tier I payback in the game (5.49 laps in 3p, 3.66 in 4p — the only Tier I that clears the 7-lap ceiling in 3-player). It is not a trap; it is one of the most efficient upgrade paths. Recommendation withdrawn.

R-04 · Mint pricing increase survives, weakened

Current: Mint 150 TN. Returns 220 (3p) / 330 (4p) over the game.

Proposed: Mint 200 TN.

Formula: Mint ROI 1.47 (3p) / 2.20 (4p) at 150 TN. At 200 TN, ROI becomes 1.10 / 1.65 — still positive but no longer the strongest standalone purchase. The Mint also depends on the Bank, which adds risk that should price in.

Confidence: medium. Old recommendation said "Mint returns 1 180 TN" which was wrong by 5×; the underlying intuition (Mint is a discount-priced combo asset) still holds, but the urgency is lower.

R-05 · Rev Debt base rents +1 survives, strengthened

Current: Continental Certificates base 2, Soldier Pay base 4. Set ROI 0.30 (3p) / 0.45 (4p). Tier I payback 11.67 laps (3p) / 7.77 (4p) — both fail the 7-lap test.

Proposed: Continental Certificates base 4, Soldier Pay base 6. Set ROI 0.60 (3p) / 0.90 (4p). Tier I payback 7.0 (3p) / 4.67 (4p).

Formula: Rev Debt is the only system where even Tier I (the most efficient upgrade tier numerically) exceeds the 7-lap ceiling in 4-player. A +1 base lift across both properties brings Tier I payback under the ceiling in both player counts.

Confidence: high. The corrected math shows this is more urgent than the prior audit estimated.

R-06 · Tier III cost relief does NOT survive

The prior audit proposed reducing Tier III upgrade cost to 75 % of its listed value. Corrected math shows Tier III pays back in 0.7 – 2.3 laps across all systems — the fastest tier in the game. Reducing its cost would tip Tier III into "automatic upgrade" territory and erode the cost ladder. Recommendation withdrawn.

R-07 · Route ladder rebalance new

Current: 25 / 50 / 100 / 200 TN per landing for 1 / 2 / 3 / 4 routes. Cost 200 TN each, total 800.

Proposed: 25 / 50 / 100 / 150 TN. Drops the grand-slam payment by 25 %.

Formula: 4-route grand slam currently returns 1 600 TN (3p) / 2 400 TN (4p) — 2.0× / 3.0× cost. No other asset class clears 0.7 ROI without upgrades. Cutting the top step to 150 TN brings ROI to 1.5 / 2.25 — still the best in class but no longer the only path to a winning rent income. This also lowers the 4-player spread from 351 % toward the 80 % ceiling.

Confidence: high. The route ladder is the largest single imbalance the corrected math surfaced.

R-08 · Tier I cost relief new

Current: Tier I cost = listed upgrade value × N_props. Tier I payback exceeds 7 laps for 7 of 8 systems in 3-player.

Proposed: Tier I cost reduced to 75 % of listed value (round to 5). Tier II and III costs unchanged.

Formula: Reduces Set→I payback by 25 %; Rev Debt 3p goes 11.67 → 8.75 (still over), State Debt 8.75 → 6.56 (passes), six other systems land between 5.8 and 6.0 laps.

Confidence: medium. The economy is currently upgrade-gated in a way that may be intentional design — forcing players to commit to one or two sets they can fully tier up rather than spreading wide. Lowering Tier I cost reduces that pressure. Playtest before applying.

Path to v0.2: apply R-01, R-02, R-05, and R-07 — these have high confidence and direct math support. Hold R-04 and R-08 for playtest evidence. Withdraw R-03 and R-06.
