Solo / Digital Mode

Making the Hamilton System playable alone · concept, not implementation

Sovereign is a board game about building economic sovereignty through public credit, federal revenue, institutions, infrastructure, and industry. This document specifies a browser-based companion that makes the system playable solo while preserving the board-game soul: movement, assets, Acts, tracks, cards, economic tension, and historical causality.

§ 01Product Intent

The printable board game (v0.2 balance candidate) is the canonical object. The digital mode is its companion, not its replacement. It exists to:

  • Make the system accessible when no table, no print, or no opponents are available.
  • Automate state memory — track values, owned assets, lap count, cash, ledger — so a solo player can focus on decisions, not bookkeeping.
  • Provide scripted opponents with deterministic, traceable behavior, so a single player faces real economic pressure.
  • Surface historical causality inline as short, optional narration — never as a lecture.
  • Enable replay and simulation for balance inspection and learning the system.

What it is not. It is not a generic educational app, a trivia game, a passive simulation, or a spreadsheet front-end. The board game's grammar — square loop, dice, cards, votes, tracks — is preserved in the digital interface.

§ 02Core Loop

Plain-language loop

  1. Choose Role (Treasury Architect · State Broker · Merchant Financier · Manufacturer).
  2. Choose solo mode (Guided · Competitive · Replay).
  3. Initialize board state. Tracks: Credit 5, Resistance 2, Capacity 1.
  4. On each player's turn: roll dice → animate move → resolve space → make decisions → end turn.
  5. At the start of each lap: reveal next Act of Congress → all players vote → resolve effect if passed → adjust tracks → place Act face-up in tray.
  6. Card spaces: draw → read → resolve → adjust tracks → log.
  7. Tax / corner spaces: auto-resolve with player confirmation.
  8. End of lap 7: finish the round, compute Influence, present Endgame Report.

State-transition diagram

SETUP → [LAP_START → ACT_VOTE → ACT_RESOLVE] → TURN_LOOP[N] ↓ TURN_LOOP : ROLL → MOVE → RESOLVE_SPACE → DECISIONS → END_TURN RESOLVE : asset | route | institution | tax | card | corner DECISIONS : human paused · scripted opponents deterministic ↓ (lap ≤ 7) → next LAP_START (lap = 7) → SCORE → REPORT

§ 03Digital Mode Surfaces

Eight surfaces, organised as one persistent center (Board View) and seven sidebars / drawers that the player can open as needed. Surfaces are not modal pages — they coexist in a single viewport, with the Board View always visible.

A · Board View
The full 17 × 17 board rendered via the same SVG pipeline as the print tiles. Player tokens, owned assets marked with their owner's color, current lap, an Acts lane showing passed Acts face-up beside the board, and the three shared-track markers along the bottom.
B · Player Treasury Panel
Per-player: cash on hand, role, list of owned assets grouped by system, upgrade tokens placed, current Influence estimate (live), liabilities (mortgaged properties, owed rent), and Crisis status if applicable. The human player's panel is anchored bottom-left; opponents are collapsible cards.
C · Asset Inspector
Click an asset to open: full card detail, computed payment table (Base / Full Set / Tier I / II / III), upgrade options with cost and incremental income, Act modifiers in force, and a short historical note (40–60 words, expandable).
D · Event / Card Drawer
When a Market Shock or Republic Debate card is drawn: card fronted full-size, effect resolution buttons, track-delta preview, optional historical narration. Card stays visible until the player presses "Resolve." Discard pile inspectable.
E · Acts of Congress Panel
At lap start: top card revealed face-up. Vote tiles for each player. Human votes by click or keyboard (Y / N). Scripted opponents vote with a visible reason in the ledger ("State Broker votes YES — holds 2 State Debt"). Passed Acts move to the tray with their effects summarized.
F · Shared Tracks Panel
Public Credit, Public Resistance, Industrial Capacity — each shown as a 0–12 scale with marker, threshold flags (v0.2: Capacity ≥ 6 / ≥ 8), and a tooltip on each movement: "Capacity +2 · Report on Manufactures passed."
G · Turn Log / Ledger
Right-side scrollback of every event: cash movement, track movement, Act resolution, card draw, opponent decision with reason. Searchable, exportable, used for debugging the AI and for player learning. Lines are time-stamped to lap and turn.
H · Endgame Report
After lap 7 scoring: final Influence breakdown by source (matching the audit's scoring table), economic system the player built (which Acts they passed, which sets they completed, which tracks they pushed), strongest and weakest holding, and a 300–500 word historical narration scoped to the actual game played.
Visual style. All surfaces inherit the prototype's Federalist Treasury aesthetic: parchment + ink + system color bands + locked icon set. The same SVG symbols (debt certificate, customs house, harbor, manufactory, etc.) render at every size.

§ 04Solo Modes

A · Guided Treasury Secretary

Goal: build a stable Hamiltonian economy against scripted political and market pressure.

Agency: human plays a single role and makes all asset and vote decisions. Opponents are minimal — a scripted "Opposition" votes on every Act based on shared-track state.

Opponents: 1 scripted Opposition (votes NO on Acts that raise Resistance).

Win: finish 7 laps with Credit ≥ 8 and Capacity ≥ 6.

Loss: Default fires.

Teaches: what each Act does, how tracks couple, how upgrades pay back.

B · Competitive Solo Table

Goal: beat 2 scripted opponents on Influence at lap 7.

Agency: human plays a role of their choosing.

Opponents: 2 scripted profiles. MVP pairs Treasury / Finance + Merchant / Infrastructure.

Win: highest Influence at game end.

Loss: finish below both opponents.

Teaches: board-game strategy, route ladder pressure, the Bank Charter tipping point.

C · Simulation Replay

Goal: watch or step through an AI-only game.

Agency: none in-game. Player picks the seed, profiles, and speed.

Opponents: 3–4 scripted profiles, all AI.

Win: n/a.

Loss: n/a.

Teaches: economy inspection. Used by the designer for balance work and by the player to study system interactions.

§ 06State Model

Conceptual pseudo-schema. Not implementation code. The state model is the contract between game logic, opponent AI, ledger, and save/load.

GameState = {
  rngSeed: integer,                  // deterministic replay
  lap: 1..7,
  turn: 1..players.length,
  phase: 'lap-start' | 'turn' | 'act-vote' | 'scoring' | 'over',
  players: [
    {
      id, name, role: 'treasury-architect' | 'state-broker' |
                      'merchant-financier' | 'manufacturer',
      profile: 'human' | 'treasury' | 'merchant' | 'manufacturer' | 'opportunist',
      position: 0..39,
      cash: integer,
      assets: [ { kind: 'property'|'route'|'institution', id, tier: 0..3,
                  mortgaged: boolean } ],
      influencePoints: integer,        // accumulated game-end-only at score
      crisis: { active: boolean, turnsRemaining: integer },
      bankruptLaps: integer,
      keptCards: [ cardId ]            // "You Are Hamilton"
    }
  ],
  decks: {
    marketShock: { draw: [cardId], discard: [cardId] },
    republicDebate: { draw: [cardId], discard: [cardId] }
  },
  acts: {
    sequence: [ act1..act7 ],            // current pending order
    pending: actId | null,
    passed: [ { actId, lap, effectsInForce: [...] } ],
    failed: [ { actId, lap } ]           // moved to bottom of sequence
  },
  tracks: {
    credit:    { value: 0..12, lastDelta: { source, delta, lap } },
    resistance:{ value: 0..12, lastDelta: { ... } },
    capacity:  { value: 0..12, lastDelta: { ... } }
  },
  ledger: [
    { lap, turn, actor, event, detail, cashDelta?, trackDelta? }
  ]
}

§ 07Automation Rules

Automated (no human input)

  • Dice roll · 2d6, RNG-seeded for replay.
  • Movement · token slides along board path.
  • Rent calculation · base × tier × act multipliers.
  • Tax space payments (Federal Excise, Speculation Scandal).
  • Route ladder lookup (v0.2: 25 / 50 / 100 / 150).
  • Institution dice payments (Bank pre/post-charter, Mint state machine).
  • Card effect resolution where unambiguous.
  • Act timing · revealed at lap start.
  • Scripted opponent votes, bids, upgrades.
  • Track threshold firings (Default, Rebellion, Capacity bonuses).
  • Bankruptcy bookkeeping.
  • Influence scoring at game end.
  • Ledger writes for every state transition.

Player choice (no automation)

  • Role selection at game start.
  • Solo mode selection.
  • Buy / decline an unowned asset on landing.
  • Bid in auctions (snap-bid UI: pass · +10 · +50 · +100 · custom).
  • Upgrade decisions: which property, which tier, when.
  • Mortgage decisions.
  • Vote on Acts of Congress (Yes / No / Pay 100 TN to force).
  • Card-driven choices ("Strict Construction Objection" → Crisis OR pay 50).
  • Crisis exit choice (pay 50 · roll doubles · skip turn).
  • End of turn confirmation.
  • v2: trades with opponents.
Anti-drift rule. Automation must never make a decision the board game would force a player to make. If the rule book gives the player a choice, the digital mode pauses for it — even if the "obviously correct" answer exists. Automation is for bookkeeping, not strategy.

§ 08Historical Narration Layer

Narration teaches Hamilton's system without becoming a lecture. Default snippet: 40 – 60 words. One paragraph, renders inline. An Expand button reveals 150 – 200 words of deeper context. Endgame report is a single 300 – 500 word section scoped to the player's actual game.

Triggers

  • First purchase of each system (one-shot per game, per player).
  • Each Act of Congress when it passes.
  • Each shared-track threshold first reached.
  • Default · Rebellion · Bank Charter · Coinage Act · Report on Manufactures (special-cased).
  • Endgame report (always).

Sample Snippets

Revolutionary Debt · first purchase

In 1789 Continental Certificates and Soldier Pay Notes traded at five to twenty cents on the dollar. Hamilton's First Report proposed honoring them all at par. Speculators ran ahead of post riders; veterans cried betrayal; the federal Treasury bought its first political ally.
▸ Expand (160 words)

Assumption Act passes

The federal government has assumed the war debts of every state. Massachusetts and South Carolina creditors are now federal creditors. The compromise that passed it moved the capital to the Potomac — Virginia's price for the deal.
▸ Expand (185 words)

Bank Charter passes

The Bank of the United States is open. Federally chartered, partly federally owned, operated as a commercial bank. It will hold Treasury deposits, discount merchant bills, and issue banknotes that circulate at par. Jefferson called it unconstitutional. Washington signed it anyway.
▸ Expand (200 words on implied powers)

Tariff Schedule passes

A duty on every imported barrel, yard of British cloth, and cask of West India rum. The federal Treasury now has a dependable revenue stream. Merchants pass the cost to consumers; importers grumble; the Treasury's books begin to balance.
▸ Expand (170 words)

Report on Manufactures passes

Hamilton's Report on Manufactures argues that strategic industry — armories, foundries, shipyards, textile works — is a national-security project, not a private affair. Congress shelved most of it. The framework outlived the politics.
▸ Expand (190 words on infant-industry protection)

Whiskey Rebellion / Resistance ≥ 8

The Whiskey Excise of 1791 has provoked the frontier. Western Pennsylvania farmers refuse to pay; tax collectors are tarred; federal authority is tested for the first time. Hamilton will ride west with the militia.
▸ Expand (175 words)

Industrial Capacity threshold ≥ 6

The young republic's textile mills, iron foundries, and glassworks have reached a productive threshold. Carded cotton replaces British imports; ironmongers cast plows and cannon; the country's manufactures clear a quarter of the federal market.
▸ Expand (160 words)

Tone & Length Discipline

  • Active voice. Specific dates and names where they ground the moment.
  • No "the player" framing. The narration speaks to the republic, not to the user.
  • No multi-paragraph snippets in the default state.
  • Expand is one click; never auto-opens.
  • Endgame report can be longer but is single-section, scoped to the game just finished.

§ 09Save / Load · Replay

  • Local browser save — autosave to localStorage after every turn. One slot per browser by default; named slots in v2.
  • Export JSON — full GameState including rngSeed and ledger. Downloadable as sovereign-game-{seed}.json.
  • Import JSON — drag & drop or file picker. Resumes from any saved state.
  • Replay ledger — re-runs the game deterministically from rngSeed + decision log. Lets the designer step through any past play to inspect AI behavior.
  • Rewind one turn — v2 only. Reverts the last ledger block. Used for misclicks, not for cheating.
  • Deterministic seed — for the simulation mode and for shared scenario challenges later.

§ 11MVP — Smallest Version That Preserves the Soul

Player configuration: 1 human + 2 scripted opponents = 3-player game (matches the prototype's lower player bound). MVP opponent pairing: Treasury / Finance + Merchant / Infrastructure — the two strongest profiles from FC-EM-002. v2 expands to 4 players with all four profiles selectable.

MVP must include

  • Automated board movement, dice, and rent calculation.
  • Automated Acts of Congress (reveal, vote, resolve, place in tray).
  • Automated shared tracks (Credit · Resistance · Capacity), including v0.2 thresholds.
  • Card draw and effect resolution for both decks.
  • Asset purchase, decline, auction, and upgrade decisions exposed as human choices.
  • The two scripted opponent profiles with deterministic, traceable behavior.
  • Endgame Influence report.
  • Turn log / ledger surface visible at all times.
  • The accessibility floor described in § 15.

MVP must prove

  • Solo play is possible — a player can complete a 7-lap game with no physical components.
  • Hamilton's economic system is legible — the player can answer "what did each Act do?" by playing.
  • Shared tracks influence decisions — the player can see why they shift and respond to thresholds.
  • Scripted opponents create pressure — the route-runaway and the Treasury-stack profiles both feel real.

§ 15Accessibility

The digital mode inherits the prototype's color-blind and grayscale fallbacks (every system carries a unique pattern + icon + label, not color alone). Additional requirements at MVP, not v2:

  • Keyboard navigation for the core loop: Roll (Space) · Buy / Decline (Y / N) · Vote (Y / N) · Pay-to-force (F) · End Turn (Enter) · Open inspector (I) · Open ledger (L). Tab cycles through interactive elements in DOM order.
  • Focus indicators on every interactive element. 2 px outline, ink color, no opacity tricks.
  • Screen-reader meaningful labels on board spaces ("Space 14 · Whiskey Excise · Revenue System · cost 160 TN · base rent 12"), cards (full effect text), tracks (current value as text, threshold proximity), and opponent decisions (full ledger line read aloud on focus).
  • Track values readable as text, not only as marker position. "Public Credit: 7 of 12. Threshold ≥ 8 not yet reached."
  • Minimum 14 px body type at default zoom. Numbers and labels remain readable at 200 % browser zoom without horizontal scroll.
  • No flashing or rapid animation on track or event transitions. Movement uses 200 – 400 ms ease-out.
  • Reduce Motion toggle in the toolbar. Honors prefers-reduced-motion media query by default.
  • Color contrast meets WCAG AA at all surfaces. Ink #1A1612 on parchment #F0E6CD measures ~ 14:1; white text on saturated band colors also high-contrast.
