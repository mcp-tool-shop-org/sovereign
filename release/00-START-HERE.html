SOVEREIGN

The Hamilton System Board Game
Founding Credit · Fund the debt. Build the bank. Industrialize the republic.
v1.4.0 Beta · Circuit Victory + Federal Era + Chronicler

Sovereign is two artifacts and one design thesis. A printable 34-sheet board game about the founding of US public credit, frozen at its v0.2 balance baseline, and a self-contained solo / digital adaptation (v1.4.0 beta) that plays the same game locally in a browser against two deterministic scripted opponents. The digital game ends by circuit victory — when one player completes their fourth crossing of Treasury Opens — and at Final Accounting the highest-Influence player wins, not necessarily the one who got around the board first. Its v0.18 reducer mechanics are preserved byte-identical from a nine-version balance arc driven by simulation evidence, with strategic depth, a Federal Era arc, and the Chronicler historical voice layered on top.

Operating model

Local-only. No network calls. No accounts. No cloud sync. No LLM opponents. Single RNG: mulberry32(state.rngSeed). Telemetry is reporting-side and never mutates game state. The full game, all profiles, all telemetry, and all batch infrastructure live in one self-contained HTML file.

Known caveats

  • v1.4.0 is a beta. Each vertical slice — strategic depth, the Federal Era arc, the Chronicler — was structurally audited and validated against simulation diagnostics, and cold-walked at slice level, but the full four-layer stack has not been walked end-to-end by a fresh human player. Treat as opt-in until you've walked it yourself.
  • Trigger ≠ winner. The player who completes the fourth circuit only wins on Influence in about a third of games (38 / 100). This is intentional — Final Accounting rewards economic depth, not speed around the board.
  • AI opponents do not yet adapt to the v1.2–v1.4 layers. They run v0.18 decision functions — they don't race for a Vision or spend HAND cards strategically the way a human would. Real human play will diverge from the CANONICAL × 100 measurements.
  • Treasury / Finance remains intentionally strongest, within the target band. This matches the historical thesis: public credit and federal finance were Hamilton's dominant economic lever.
  • Failure events remain mostly decorative. Credit Crisis fires occasionally; Default and Rebellion almost never reach the top tiers even with more time to compound. Future versions may revisit fail-state pressure.
  • Three-profile competitive set. The Opportunist / Cash profile from the concept document is deferred. The locked lineup is Treasury / Merchant / Manufacturer.
